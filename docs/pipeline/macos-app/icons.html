★Two icons, two rule-sets

This app lives in the menu bar: press a hotkey → drag a screen region → OCR → translate. It needs two visually-related but technically-different marks. The deciding constraint for the status icon is legibility at 16 px — at that size 译's interior strokes (the 讠 radical + 睪 body) blur, so the winning concepts use a bold weight, open counters, and minimal extra ornament. Everything below renders 译 as drawn vector strokes (not a font) so the silhouette is controllable at any size.

Why 译 and not 譯

The PM chose the Simplified form. Simplified 译 (7 strokes: the two-dot 讠 speech radical + 睪) is dramatically cleaner at 16 px than Traditional 譯 (20 strokes, the full 言 + dense 睪) which collapses into a smudge in the menu bar. Simplified is the right call for a template image — fewer strokes, larger counters, survives the shrink.

1Five status-icon concepts

Each concept wraps 译 in a different container that nods to the app's job. For every one: the form at ~80 px (left), then the actual 16 px and 22 px renders sitting on a light and a dark menu-bar strip — the real test of a macOS template image. Single ink colour throughout; currentColor would be auto-supplied by AppKit, here drawn black-on-light / white-on-dark to simulate that.

2Three app-icon concepts

Full-colour squircles for the .app / Finder / .dmg, each pairing with a status concept above. Restrained palette — this is a calm utility, not a toy: warm paper, deep ink, one disciplined accent (pine or clay). Shown at ~96 px and ~256 px. Depth comes from a soft top-light gradient on the squircle material plus a single layered shadow, never a rainbow.

APP ICON 1 · recommended

Paper card · pine capture-frame

Pairs with status A/C. Cream face + ink 译 mirrors the app's own paper UI; pine corner-brackets quietly carry the "screen capture" idea without a literal camera. Lightest, most utility-like.

APP ICON 2

Pine field · paper bubble

Pairs with status B. Confident single-accent square; the paper speech-bubble reads as "language" instantly and the pine reads premium. Highest colour presence in the Dock.

APP ICON 3

Pairs with status D. Dark-luxury restraint: clay 译 glows against ink, faint globe meridians sit behind it. Most distinctive at large sizes; the lightest-touch globe avoids the "two competing marks" trap of status D.

3Recommended pairing

Ship Status A (rounded-square 译) + App Icon 1 (paper card)

The status icon's only job is to be unmistakable at 16 px, and A wins that test. The badge keyline is a single thick rounded rectangle that stays crisp when everything else blurs, and inside it 译 keeps its widest possible counters because nothing else is sharing the box. On both the light and dark strips above, A's silhouette stays balanced and centred — no tail, no second glyph, no fine ticks to disappear. It also visually rhymes with App Icon 1 (same square-holds-译 idea), giving the app one coherent identity from menu bar to Dock to .dmg.

If the PM wants the capture gesture foregrounded, Status C + App Icon 1 is the strong fallback — C's corner brackets survive 16 px better than D's globe or E's slash, and App 1 already carries the same pine brackets, so the pair is even tighter conceptually. Keep B as the warmest option if the brand wants "language" over "capture".

Honest 16 px verdict — what fails

Concept E (译 / A split) fails at 16 px. Three marks (译 + slash + A) in a 16-pixel box leaves each one starved; the slash and the A's negative space muddy into the 译 strokes and the result reads as an ink blob. Keep E for marketing/onboarding art only. Concept D (译 + globe) is borderline — the corner globe is legible at 22 px but at 16 px it degrades into a second smudge competing with 译; usable only if the globe is dropped and 译 fills the frame. Concept B is fine at 22 px but the bubble tail costs vertical room, slightly shrinking 译. A and C are the only two that are genuinely clean at 16 px.

4How each icon is used

  • Status icon → menu-bar template image. Export the chosen mark (A) as a 1× / 2× PDF or PNG named StatusIcon with Render As: Template Image in the asset catalog. It must be a single colour on transparency — AppKit recolours it automatically for light vs dark menu bars (the proofs above simulate that inversion). Set on NSStatusItem.button.image; do not tint it yourself.
  • App icon → asset catalog AppIcon set. The full-colour squircle (App Icon 1) ships as the standard macOS icon sizes (16→1024, 1× & 2×). macOS applies the squircle mask and Dock shadow at the system level, but provide the full-bleed art; the superellipse here matches the platform mask so the keyline never clips.
